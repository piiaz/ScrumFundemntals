Scrum Fundemntals

Scrum Values

Commitment
The team is dedicated to achieving goals and supporting each other.
Focus
Everyone concentrates on the work of the Sprint and the goals of the Scrum Team.
Courage
The team has the bravery to do the right thing and tackle tough problems.
Openness
Team members share progress and challenges honestly and transparently.
Respect
Everyone is valued for their skills and contributions.

These guide behavior and decision-making in Scrum. Without values, the framework lacks meaning.

Scrum Pillars

Transparency
Work and progress must be visible to everyone.
Inspection
Frequent checks help detect undesirable variances.
Adaptation
Adjust processes and plans as soon as issues arise.

Enable continuous improvement through empirical process control.

Scrum Roles

Product Owner
Maximizes product value and manages the backlog.
Scrum Master
Ensures Scrum is understood and helps remove impediments.
Developers
Create the product increment during the Sprint.

Roles are essential to understanding how Scrum functions and who is responsible for what.

Scrum Events

Sprint
A time-boxed iteration to deliver usable product value.
Sprint Planning
Define what can be delivered and how to do the work.
Daily Scrum
Daily sync to inspect progress and adapt the plan.
Sprint Review
Inspect the outcome and plan future adaptations.
Sprint Retrospective
Reflect and improve teamwork and processes.

These events create rhythm and enable Scrum Pillars.

Scrum Artifacts

Product Backlog
Ordered list of everything known to be needed in the product.
Sprint Backlog
Work selected for the Sprint plus a plan to deliver it.
Increment
The sum of all completed work usable by the customer.

Artifacts provide transparency and a shared source of truth.

Scrum Artifacts Commitments

Product Goal
A long-term objective for the product.
Sprint Goal
A single objective for the Sprint.
Definition of Done
A shared understanding of what makes work complete.

Helps connect artifacts to objectives and purpose.
